Supply and demand are two fundamental concepts in economics that determine the prices of goods and services in a market.

Supply refers to the quantity of a product or service that producers are willing and able to offer for sale at a given price level. It's like a basket of apples - the more apples you have, the more you can sell.

Demand, on the other hand, refers to the quantity of a product or service that consumers are willing and able to buy at a given price level. It's like a hungry person looking for an apple - the more hungry they are, the more apples they'll buy.

When the supply of a product is greater than the demand for it, the price tends to decrease. This is like having too many apples and not enough people to buy them, so the price goes down.

On the other hand, when the demand for a product is greater than the supply, the price tends to increase. This is like having a limited number of apples and a lot of people wanting to buy them, so the price goes up.
